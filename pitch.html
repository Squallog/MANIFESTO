MANIFESTO

The Pitch

The short documentary will explore the various forms of companionship provided by dogs. It will begin as a montage, presenting various people going about their lives with their companions, but will slowly build towards the core moment which will be uncovered at the end.

The moment in question will be an inception - either the birth of a new litter, or the adoption of a dog by a new family. In concluding with actuality drawn from a moment of inception, the peice will gain a cyclical structure - reflecting the historic link between mankind and its affinity for dogs.

The Challenge

We considered this idea initially, but decided to shelve it due to logistical complexity surrounding the key moment of actuality. There are major challenges associated with attempting to film a whelping process - strangers with film equipment can easily spook or stress out a whelping dog, and with the whelping process being so unpredictable, we'd need to have a camera available and ready to get on-location 24/7 during the leadup to the shot.

Addressing these challenges, we could instead shoot the introduction of a dog in a shelter to a new home - this could lend itself to themes tackling the tendancy for people and their dogs to lead each other through moments of crisis - however there is still the challenge of having to find a shelter with an adoption taking place during the production perod.

What's Changed?

At the time of the discussion in which we chose to shelve this idea, we had been given a grade of 65% for our film-jam work. This left minimal room for for risk and nessecitated the development of a safe, reliable idea that could be executed with few logistical challenges. Under this framework, it was decided that the story of Darryl Kennedy was most appropriate.

On the day prior to the presentation pitch, however, the revised grades were released - we had been pushed up to 75%. At 75% there is considerable wiggle room to compensate for setbacks in subsequent assignments. To this end, the criterion that led us to settle on Daryrl's story are no longer relevant.

The Next Steps

I've sent out feeler emails and forum posts to dog shelters and breeder groups with the aim of finding a breeder or shelter who would be happy to colaborate with the project.

Other footage featured throughout the doc will focus on people from all manner of backgrounds going about their day-to-day lives in the companionship of their dogs. These shots will take an observational inspired form, with minimal dialogue or direct interaction with contributors - these shots will be designed to higlight the often overlooked but ever-present signifficance of dogs in our society.
